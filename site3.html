It would take thousands of years for the bread made by prehistoric humans 30,000 years ago to become what it is today. There are three main factors that makes bread what it is today and one of them is fermentation. This process was first found by the Ancient Egyptians through an accident, very much like how bread itself was discovered. The process involved microorganisms breaking down substances and releasing carbon dioxide as a byproduct. This would create the loaves of fluffy and airy bread that we see on the shelves of the supermarket today. At first this process was only viable through natural means but as time went on, people would develop more efficient and advance methods for this step in making bread. The second major factor was an improved quality of flour. Early on in the history of bread, grains would be grounded by hand with a rock but in 800 BC, Mesopotamians found a method of refining this process and making it more effecient. This refined method yielded finer and whiter flour which would be highly desired. This desire for whiter flour would be a trend in society until recently. Nowadays health fads have made whole grain bread far more popular than it was in the past. The rich that use to buy white bread now buy whole grain bread while the poor that used to buy whole grain bread now buy white bread. The third and final factor contributing to how our bread is made today is mechanized slicing. Otto Rohwedder, a jeweler, created the first mechanized slicing. While the idea did not garner much support in he beginning, it would eventually become successful and 90% of bread sold in stores would be sliced. Due to these three factors the white loaves of sliced bread are abundant in American supermarkets and has become a big part of American culture. Looking back on the origins of bread, this food sources had evolved greatly from it's humble beginnings as gruel.
